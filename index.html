The Engineer

Hi!

I am an undergraduate student at UC Berkeley studying Economics and Computer Science with an emphasis in Electrical Engineering. Originally I am from Armenia, but I completed high school at Chinese International School in Singapore. I am broadly interested in Labor and Behavioral Economics, Graphy Theory, Computer Vision, Digital and Analog Integrated Circuit Design, Networks, and Signal Processing. My current projects are making a quadcopter with a closed-loop control system, designing microprocessors based on the RISC-V instructions set, and building a card counting simulator for Blackjack for commercial use. A list of past projects can be found here.

In life, I enjoy reading; my favorite piece of literature is "1984" by George Orwell. From academic readings, I find Donald Knuth's "The Art of Computer Programming" thrilling and captivating. I am an active runner and believe investment in health is the best type of investment. Incidentally, I am a passive investor. If you are one too, I suggest only investing in index funds. I play electric guitar and occasionally perform for family and friends.
